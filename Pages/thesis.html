Research Questions

  1. How do university students with dyslexia use generative AI for academic support?
  2. What benefits and limitations do students experience when using general-purpose AI tools?
  3. How does AI affect students' workflows, confidence, independence, and approaches to learning?

Project Overview

This master's thesis investigates how university students with dyslexia use general-purpose generative AI tools to support their academic work. The research explores how AI is incorporated into tasks such as reading, writing, organizing, and learning, as well as the benefits, limitations, and concerns students experience.

Status: Research in progress

Methods: Literature review, semi-structured interviews, transcription, coding, and thematic analysis

The Research Gap

Students with dyslexia may experience challenges with academic reading, writing, note-taking, and organization. While assistive technologies have been developed to support these tasks, less is known about how students use general-purpose generative AI tools, such as ChatGPT, in their everyday academic work.

This study examines whether students are adapting these tools into informal support systems, even though they were not specifically designed for dyslexia.

Research Approach

I am using a qualitative research approach to understand students' lived experiences rather than measure predefined outcomes. Semi-structured interviews allow participants to describe how dyslexia affects their academic work and how AI fits into their individual needs, strategies, and routines.

The research is informed by theories of affordance and technology appropriation. These concepts help explain what students believe AI enables them to do and how they adapt a general-purpose technology to meet their own needs.

Interview Focus

The interviews explore several areas:

Academic experiences related to dyslexia
Previous forms of support and assistive technology
AI tools used by participants
Academic tasks supported by AI
Changes to workflows and study practices
Benefits, frustrations, and limitations
Trust, privacy, and university regulations
Effects on confidence, independence, and learning

Current Progress

Interviews and transcription have been completed, and the study is currently in the analysis phase. I am coding the interview transcripts and comparing participants' experiences to identify recurring patterns in how AI is used, where it provides support, and where it creates new challenges.

Because analysis is still underway, the themes and conclusions presented in the final thesis may continue to develop.
